Publicly publish the harm done to BIPOC staff as well as publicly outlining methods of change﻿.

Incorporate BIPOC staff in the rewriting of existing firm internal policies to better represent themselves﻿

Commit to providing required in house training and/or funding for all staff to be trained in critical race theory, design justice and community engagement.﻿
Accountability

  • Provide public salary ranges in job descriptions and public evaluation criteria for hiring and promotions, and share this information with your network
  • Commit to no unpaid internships

Publish salaries of all firm employees, ensure BIPOC staff are paid equally to white counterparts, and publicly state commitment to pay equity; include this language in employee offer letters/contracts.

Establish clear, publicly accessible executive standards on anti-racist practices and process for removal of executives for any violation of these standards﻿.

  • Publicly commit to mandatory annual critical race theory, design justice and community engagement training as a requirement for all senior-level positions
  • Open up the firm to a community audit of projects, staffing and community engagement.
Representation

Hire and actively recruit BIPOC designers﻿.

Promote and maintain BIPOC leadership.

Establish a worker cooperative or worker empowered practice model﻿.

Establish BIPOC funded fellowship programming for sustaining BIPOC recruitment and promotion; share and advocate for the integration of intergenerational knowledge systems in other design institutions.
Reparations

Provide immediate reparative compensation to BIPOC staff to match white staff salaries﻿.

Redistribute a percentage of firm revenue to BIPOC staff (i.e. bonuses) and provide additional salaried compensation for BIPOC.

Pay for continuing education and provide additional monetary incentives for BIPOC staff to elevate experience and position within the firm. Provide paid time off to volunteer and engage in local initiatives.

Create a BIPOC-led and majority internal team that leads the firm in establishing where monies are focused and spent internally and externally on underserved and BIPOC-focused initiatives﻿.
Accessibility

Provide paid time and safe space for BIPOC staff to express thoughts on firm management and leadership.

Develop programs and policies to retain BIPOC employees within the firm. Retention efforts should include developing a mentorship program and support for professional licensure.

Address and incorporate feedback from BIPOC staff on management and leadership, and institutionalize routine office-wide, transparent, leadership reviews.

  • Restructure firm leadership to become employee-owned with equal input on decision-making and investment.
  • Develop pathways for project and firm leadership from the community.
  • Develop a cohort of design advocate (community activist, advocates, design justice researchers) roles integral to the firm and its growth.
Influence

Incorporate an anti-racist evaluation as part of the hiring process﻿.

Create an anti-racist resource library and institutionalize weekly mandatory readings for all staff. This includes allocating paid time to read and discuss the knowledge gained from these resources.

  • Establish contractually bound rules barring the firm from blocking any potential union development within the firm.
  • Develop clear leadership pathways that recognize the inherent barriers for BIPOC employees.

  • Develop principal and partner level leadership roles within an employee-owned practice that are directly bound to the Design Justice Demands.
  • Commit to only working with firms that implement anti-racist practices sharing the experiences of the firm's journey towards anti-racism, and publicly presenting or workshopping a guide with lessons learned to the broader industry

Leadership & Membership
Acknowledgement

Publicly acknowledge the lack of BIPOC representation and the organization's racist practices based on feedback provided directly by existing BIPOC staff.

Publicly publish the harm done to BIPOC staff and publicly outline methods for change.

Incorporate BIPOC staff and members in the rewriting of the existing organization's internal policies to better represent BIPOC voices.

Hold routine workshops and other outreach programs to educate staff and members on anti-racist practices.
Accountability

Acknowledge institutional biases to force structural changes within existing policies and procedures, and to promote outcomes of equity as opposed to assimilation.

Create a safe space for BIPOC members to share experiences. Establish a platform for these experiences to be heard and addressed by people in positions of power.﻿

Establish clear, publicly accessible executive standards on anti-racist practices and process for removal of executives for any violation of these standards.

Hire an external compliance team to create new organizational structures and monitoring methods to ensure injustices are not repeated.﻿
Representation

Hire and maintain a proportional amount of BIPOC within organization.

Place designers on teams that match the demographics of the neighboring community and populations affected by organization's projects or research, especially in areas with predominantly BIPOC residents.

Encourage non-BIPOC leaders to step down to allow space for BIPOC leadership.

Nominate BIPOC members to leadership positions and provide transparent pathways to leadership to ensure underrepresented voices continue to be heard.
Reparations

Publish salaries for the organization to ensure BIPOC staff are paid equally to white counterparts and provide reparative compensation to BIPOC staff.

Provide free memberships to BIPOC community members.

Provide additional compensation to BIPOC staff in the form of continuing education and provide additional monetary incentives for BIPOC staff to elevate experience and position within the organization. Provide paid time off to staff to volunteer and engage in local initiatives.

Create a BIPOC-led and majority membership team that leads the organization in establishing where funds are focused and spent internally and externally on underserved and BIPOC-focused initiatives.﻿
Accessibility

Invest in retaining BIPOC membership within the organization. Retention efforts should include: developing a mentorship program, providing free memberships, and creating free workshops/panel access.﻿

Incorporate the ideas and opinions shared and voiced by BIPOC staff and members into future programming and initiatives.

Make room for BIPOC in leadership by expanding the budget to accommodate more BIPOC in leadership through altering the salaries of current non-BIPOC in leadership to free up money for new positions.

Collaboratively develop and establish a non-hierarchical organization through the creation of a horizontal internal structure.
Influence

Incorporate an anti-racist evaluation as part of the staff hiring and membership application process to ensure racist people are rejected.

Create a free anti-racist resource library for staff and members and conduct free public forums for education and open dialogue.﻿

Provide public programming on a regular schedule, outlining anti-racist practices and methodology for within the organization, as well as for outreach in the greater community via the organization's projects and programs.

Maintain an anti-racism working group within the organization that educates members and extended networks on past injustices, sustains anti-racist initiatives, and keeps leadership, staff, members, and partner organizations accountable.

Academic Instituitons
Leadership & Staff
Acknowledgement

Publicly acknowledge the lack of representation and how the institution has been complicit in systemic racism based on feedback provided directly by BIPOC staff and student workers. This includes the evaluation of the institution's pedagogy, hiring practices, and student recruitment.

Provide recurring anti-racist training and professional development courses for faculty.

Require all faculty to present their understanding of racism in the built environment, urban policy, and design culture through their practice, research, continuing education, and curriculum.

Provide regular public programming outlining anti-racist practices and methodologies within the institution.
Accountability

Make actions, decisions, and conversations held at the leadership level accessible to the entire institutional body. This includes publishing meeting minutes, agendas, recorded meeting sessions and providing summary reports of actions to the student body and staff.

  • Publish a list of who is funding the institution's curricula, facilities, and scholarships, and identify which of those sources support racist agendas. Work to replace those funding sources.
  • Publish all faculty salaries and ensure all BIPOC affiliates are being rightly paid for their time.

  • Publicly publish hiring practices and criteria for evaluating potential hires.
  • Include student input in the decision making of new hires, tenures, and leadership promotions.

Partner with existing anti-racist organizations to co-create a new organizational structure that serves as a permanent structure for accountability.
Representation

Evaluate the current racial make-up of the department and make this information easily accessible to the public.

  • Elevate faculty and staff who already have expertise in social justice, community development and other anti-racism work.
  • Join the network of educators to provide content for Design Justice curricula in Dark Matter University.

Recruit and hire BIPOC faculty, fellows, and staff, including those from misrepresented communities of color, local leaders in social justice, and provide fellowship opportunities for designers/planners with focuses in social justice.

Promote BIPOC to department chairs and higher-level positions. The institution's leadership should reflect the diversity of the populations the design field works with.
Reparations

Provide recurring funding for local community development projects led by BIPOC-led non-profits.

Ethically research and evaluate the harm the institution has caused in its surrounding BIPOC communities in conjunction with building relationships with local community leaders.

Develop programs and policies with local community leaders to rectify the harm inflicted by the institution on its surrounding BIPOC communities. This initiative should be led by students, staff, and social justice and community development departments within the institution.

Provide education awards for all BIPOC alumni pursuing licensure, and students participating in the institution's anti-racist work.
Accessibility

Create a safe environment for BIPOC faculty and staff to speak out against racism and discrimination experienced at the institution.

Develop ground rules and disciplinary action for interactions during meetings and on various communication platforms to combat discrimination against BIPOC faculty.

Restructure the hiring criteria for faculty, tenure, and leadership and creating pathways for promotion by thoroughly evaluating existing racist hiring practices.

Provide ongoing resources and capital for BIPOC faculty to support their research, professional development, and practice.
Influence

Incorporate an anti-racist evaluation as part of the staff hiring application process to reject racist people.

Create a free anti-racist resource library for staff and students, and conduct free public forums for education and open dialogue.

Schedule monthly, transparent faculty, leadership, and staff reviews to discuss any feedback on performance and individual experiences, ask any on-boarding and logistical questions, and share helpful anti-racist resources and educational exercises.

Maintain a department of anti-racism within the institution that educates on past injustices, sustains anti-racist initiatives, and keeps leadership, staff, and students accountable.

Local Governments
Leadership & Staff
Acknowledgement

Publicly acknowledge the lack of representation and how the department has been complicit in systemic racism based on feedback provided directly by BIPOC staff and the outside community.

Train department staff to identify and the effects of institutional racism by implementing a racial equity framework developed by BIPOC-led social justice organizations. Create a shared understanding among employees regarding goals for anti-racist practices.

Incorporate BIPOC staff and local community leaders in the rewriting of the existing department's internal policies to better represent BIPOC voices.

Outline fundamental changes to the department's overall structure and culture to create a more inclusive and just environment, while consulting BIPOC members throughout the process.
Accountability

Outline and publish explicit directives to address racist shortcomings within the department to meet the needs of BIPOC staff. This includes all budget requests, hiring, and promotion practices.

Create a safe space within the department for BIPOC staff to share experiences. Establish a platform for these experiences to be heard and addressed by people in positions of power.

Create more opportunities for department directors to build relationships and liaise with grassroots organizations and organizers. Create a platform for organizers to share their views on the department's practices.

Partner with existing anti-racist organizations to co-create a new organizational structure that serves as a permanent structure for accountability.
Representation

Evaluate the current racial make-up of the department and make this information easily accessible to the public.

Recruit and hire BIPOC departmental staff.

Focus efforts on recruiting city department staff from within traditionally disinvested areas to work in public service.﻿

Maintain a system that provides stable investment for recruiting, nominating and hiring BIPOC to leadership positions to ensure misrepresented voices are heard.
Reparations

Evaluate salary ranges for department staff to ensure they are comparable to those for similar roles in similar sized cities, and ensure BIPOC staff are fairly compensated compared to their white counterparts.

Provide additional salaried compensation to BIPOC staff to ensure retention and affirm their value within the department.

  • Pay for continuing education and provide additional financial incentives for BIPOC staff to elevate experience and position within the department.
  • Provide paid time off for staff to volunteer and become engaged in local initiative

Create a BIPOC-led internal department that leads the agency in establishing where funds are invested with a focus on misrepresented communities and equitable initiatives.
Accessibility

Create a safe space for BIPOC staff to speak out against racism and discrimination experienced at the department and promote agency to reconstruct procedures to ensure a just work environment.

Create partnerships with the local public for paid summer internships that empower students to be champions of their communities and build a pipeline for diversifying the planning profession.﻿

  • Make room for BIPOC leaders within the department by expanding the budget to accommodate more BIPOC leadership positions.
  • Alter the salaries of current non-BIPOC leaders to free up money for BIPOC leadership positions.

Establish a non-hierarchical organization chart to move away from "positions of power" and to supportive structures of communal working groups. Incorporate existing grassroots organizations and elected at-large council representation in the working groups.
Influence

Conduct regular organizational assessments by a paid, external anti-racist consultant to evaluate the department's practices. Publish evaluations and findings to the public.

Change existing hiring and retention policies/structure. Provide concrete steps with a timeline for implementation and incorporate public input.

Maintain a group focused on anti-racism within the department that sustains equitable initiatives, reviews internal policy changes, and keeps leadership and staff accountable.

Redraw district boundaries to ensure equitable funding across the city. Eliminate district-based council representation to provide incentives for stronger collaboration and the collective good of the city.
